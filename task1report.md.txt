To pull together this "Digital Literacy Essentials" presentation, I used a modern design tool to build a deck that felt both current and visually engaging. My goal was to create a look that actually reinforces the skills we need to navigate today’s tech-driven world. I went with vibrant, inclusive vector illustrations showing diverse groups of people collaborating on laptops and tablets which I felt really captured the social side of digital literacy. The layout itself is structured to guide the viewer through the basics—starting with smart note-taking, moving into the big "must-haves" of internet safety, and ending with how to maintain a professional digital footprint.

Honestly, the hardest part of the whole process was the conceptual side of the layout. I faced a real challenge trying to figure out the visual flow so the graphics would complement the educational text rather than just drowning it out. Deciding on a specific illustration style and then constantly tweaking the elements to lead the audience's eye required a lot of back-and-forth. It’s surprisingly easy to let a "cool" design overpower the actual message. Finding that sweet spot where the slides felt both informative and aesthetically cohesive was a massive learning experience for me, but it definitely made the final result much stronger and more professional.
